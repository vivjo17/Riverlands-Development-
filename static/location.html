Connected to Houston

Nestled just off the newly expanded Meridiana Parkway, Riverlands offers residents easy access to SH 288 and a direct 22‑mile commute to Downtown Houston, including the Texas Medical Center—blending suburban peace with major metropolitan convenience.

Growth in Motion

The area around Riverlands is rapidly evolving into a vibrant residential hub. Nearby Meridiana ranks among the nation’s fastest-growing master-planned communities and recently opened new campuses like Meridiana Elementary, Barbara Bennett Elementary, Jackie Doucet Caffey Junior High, and the adjacent Iowa Colony High School. Grand Parkway (SH 99): A Game-Changer for Connectivity

The Grand Parkway (State Highway 99) is a future outer-loop highway that will significantly improve regional access. Segment B, stretching from I‑45 South to SH 288 near Rosharon, is environmentally approved and slated for construction to begin in Spring 2027, with completion anticipated by 2030. ABC13 Houston +6 Texas Department of Transportation +6 Wikipedia +6 Notably, the planned route passes just south of Riverlands, positioning Riverlands to benefit from major new transit infrastructure.

The surrounding area is poised for rapid growth with new schools, retail, and neighboring master-planned communities on the horizon. Riverlands provides a peaceful retreat that remains close to essential conveniences and future amenities.

Community & Education Highlights Iowa Colony High School opened in 2022 and serves roughly 1,300 students with a student-teacher ratio of around 16:1. Alvin ISD continues to expand in the region, managing new elementary and junior high campuses to meet rising demand. The broader area boasts strong public school rankings, including Meridiana Elementary (A- grade) and other top-rated schools in Alvin ISD.
