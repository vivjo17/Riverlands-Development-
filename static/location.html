Connected to Houston

Nestled just off the newly expanded Meridiana Parkway, Riverlands offers residents easy access to SH 288 and a direct 22‑mile commute to Downtown Houston, including the Texas Medical Center—blending suburban peace with major metropolitan convenience.

Growth in Motion

The area surrounding Riverlands is rapidly evolving into a vibrant residential hub. Nearby Meridiana ranks among the nation’s fastest-growing master-planned communities.

Recent and planned school openings include:

  • Meridiana Elementary and Barbara Bennett Elementary
  • Jackie Doucet Caffey Junior High
  • Iowa Colony High School (opened 2022)
  • New Alvin ISD junior high (opened 2024)

These developments ensure strong infrastructure for growing families.

Grand Parkway

The future Grand Parkway (SH 99) will pass just south of Riverlands.

Segment B—from I‑45 South to SH 288 near Rosharon—is environmentally approved with construction planned for 2027 and completion by 2030, unlocking convenient regional access.

As the corridor develops, new retail and master-planned communities will bring expanded amenities while Riverlands retains a peaceful retreat close to Houston.

Community

Iowa Colony offers strong public schools and expanding amenities.

  • Iowa Colony High School serves roughly 1,300 students with a 16:1 ratio.
  • Alvin ISD continues to add elementary and junior high campuses to meet rising demand.
  • Meridiana Elementary and other top-rated schools in Alvin ISD hold A-grade rankings.

The broader area is poised for growth with new schools and retail on the horizon.

Why Riverlands Stands Out

  • Quick, stress-free access to Houston’s major job centers via Meridiana Parkway and SH 288.
  • Best-in-class educational infrastructure, with new schools already operating or under construction.
  • Future-forward connectivity thanks to the upcoming Grand Parkway, improving long-term access for residents.
  • A tranquil, low-density environment with opportunity for expansion as the area matures.
